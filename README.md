# Quality-Decision-Making-and-Knowledge-Management-QDMKM

## Overview

This repository documents my academic learning from the course **Quality Decision Making and Knowledge Management (QDMKM)** in the **MSc Business Information Systems program at FHNW – University of Applied Sciences and Arts Northwestern Switzerland**.

The course focuses on how organizations improve decision quality by combining **data-driven approaches, knowledge management practices, and artificial intelligence technologies**. It explores how structured knowledge systems and modern AI-based tools support strategic and operational decision-making.


## Key Topics Covered

### Decision Making

The course examined structured approaches to decision making in organizations. Effective decisions require evaluating alternatives, analyzing available data, and selecting the most appropriate course of action based on reliable information.

Important aspects of decision management include:

- identifying decision problems  
- evaluating alternatives  
- assessing risks and uncertainties  
- supporting decisions with analytical tools  
- improving decision quality through structured decision processes  


### Data-Driven Decision Making

Organizations increasingly rely on **data-driven decision making** to improve business outcomes. Data analytics and data mining techniques help discover patterns and insights from large datasets.

These approaches help organizations:

- identify trends and relationships in data  
- support forecasting and predictive analysis  
- reduce uncertainty in decision processes  
- improve strategic and operational planning  


### Knowledge Management

Knowledge management focuses on capturing, organizing, and sharing knowledge within organizations so that valuable insights can be reused effectively.

Key elements include:

- knowledge creation and acquisition  
- knowledge storage and documentation  
- knowledge sharing across teams and departments  
- applying knowledge to support decision-making and innovation  


### Artificial Intelligence in Decision Support

Artificial Intelligence (AI) plays an important role in modern decision-support systems. AI technologies allow organizations to analyze large volumes of data and generate insights that assist decision-makers in complex environments.

AI systems can support:

- automated decision support  
- intelligent information retrieval  
- predictive analytics  
- knowledge discovery  


### Large Language Models (LLM)

Large Language Models are advanced AI systems capable of understanding and generating natural language. They can analyze textual information, summarize documents, and support knowledge management and decision-support systems.

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Retrieval-Augmented Generation combines language models with external knowledge sources such as databases or knowledge repositories. This architecture improves the accuracy and reliability of AI-generated responses by retrieving relevant information before generating outputs.


### Ontologies and Knowledge Representation

Ontologies are used to structure and represent knowledge within organizations. They define relationships between concepts and enable systems to organize information in a meaningful and interpretable way.

Ontology-based knowledge systems support:

- semantic search  
- knowledge discovery  
- intelligent information retrieval  


### Center of Excellence (CoE)

Organizations often establish a **Center of Excellence (CoE)** to develop expertise in areas such as data analytics, artificial intelligence, and knowledge management.

A CoE provides governance, standards, and strategic guidance for implementing advanced technologies and analytical capabilities within an organization.


### Stakeholders in Decision Systems

Decision-making processes involve multiple stakeholders, including managers, analysts, domain experts, IT professionals, and business users. Understanding stakeholder needs is essential for designing decision-support systems that provide valuable insights and align with organizational objectives.
